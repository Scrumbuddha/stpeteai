Our Mission

Three Pillars.
One Community.

St. Pete AI is built on the conviction that local communities need to be active participants in the AI future — not passive recipients of it.

🏫

AI Education

From beginner workshops to practitioner deep dives — we build AI literacy across every level. Whether you're a student, professional, business owner, or curious neighbor, we have a path for you.

⚖️

Ethics & Safety

Responsible AI doesn't happen by accident. We convene local leaders, host ethics roundtables, and advocate for transparent, fair, and safe AI systems that serve our community's values.

🏠

Community Resources

Practical tools, curated guides, event recordings, and a network of people building with AI. No gatekeeping — free and open to every St. Pete resident.

Programs & Initiatives

How We Show Up
For St. Pete.

01

Monthly Community Meetups

A relaxed, regular gathering for anyone curious about AI — demos, discussions, and real talk about what's happening in the field. No jargon required.

02

AI Literacy Workshops

Hands-on sessions teaching practical AI skills — prompting, automation, and AI tools for business — built for non-technical participants.

03

Ethics & Policy Roundtables

Structured conversations with local leaders and technologists on the societal implications of AI in healthcare, education, and employment.

04

Free Resource Library

A curated, community-maintained collection of guides, tutorials, and tools — organized by topic and skill level and open to all.

05

Member Network

Connect with others building AI projects, learning new skills, or working on responsible AI initiatives across Tampa Bay.

Why It Matters

Local communities need a voice in AI's future.

The decisions being made today about AI — how it's trained, who it serves, what values it reflects — will shape St. Petersburg for generations. We exist to make sure our community is part of that conversation.

Every workshop we run, every roundtable we host, every resource we publish is a step toward an AI future that works for Tampa Bay.

  • No technical background required to participate
  • All events recorded and freely available
  • Members shape which programs we prioritize
  • Partnerships with local universities and schools
